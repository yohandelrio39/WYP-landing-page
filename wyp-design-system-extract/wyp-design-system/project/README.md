# WYP — Design System

> **WYP** — *Watch Your Passion.*
> Marque horlogère contemporaine basée à Genève, Suisse.
> Créée par l'agence **Hyperbien** (dimitri@hyperbien.ch).

---

## 1. About the brand

WYP is a contemporary watchmaking brand founded on a single conviction: **passion deserves to be worn with precision.** The watch is not an accessory — it's an identity object.

The brand uses **manga culture** as its first expressive territory (Phase 1, 0–5 years), while being architected from day one to outgrow it. The long-term vision (Phase 2, 5–10 years) is to establish WYP as an autonomous international watch brand whose symbol can stand alone, hosting many cultural collaborations under one stable graphic system.

### Brand architecture
- **WYP Core** — accessible collections, base of the brand, the first home for collaborations.
- **WYP Editions** — limited / numbered series, more assertive and exclusive.
- **WYP Master Series** — higher-value collections, deeper attention to materials and finishings.

### Audience
- **Phase 1 B2C** — 15–25, manga-literate, design-attentive, identity-expressive.
- **Phase 2 B2C** — 25–60, contemporary-culture sympathisers, sensitive to elevation.
- **B2B** — international rights-holders, distributors, watch & retail partners.

### Brand personality
Affirmed without being arrogant. Precise without being rigid. Assumed without being excessive. Contemporary without being ephemeral. Ambitious without being demonstrative.

### Manifeste (excerpt)
> Nous ne portons pas le temps.
> Nous portons ce qui nous anime.
> WYP transforme cette énergie en précision.
> Ce n'est pas un accessoire. C'est une affirmation.
> **Watch Your Passion.**

## 3. CONTENT FUNDAMENTALS

### Voice of the brand
The Brandguide defines four voice pillars: **Maîtrisé · Clair · Assumé · Posé** (controlled, clear, assertive, composed). And five tone qualities: **Précis · Cadré · Net · Assuré · Calme**.

In practice this means:

- **Every word is intentional.** No filler, no hype, no exclamation points. If a sentence does not advance the meaning, cut it.
- **State, don't sell.** WYP speaks to affirm, not to convince at all costs ("la marque parle pour affirmer, pas pour convaincre à tout prix"). Avoid call-to-action verbs of urgency ("Hurry", "Don't miss", "Limited time"). Prefer flat factual statements.
- **Short lines, generous returns.** Brand copy is set in short declarative lines, often one sentence per visual line, with line breaks doing the work that punctuation usually does. See the Manifeste for the canonical example.
- **Never adolescent, never cold.** Two failure modes are explicitly called out in the brandbook. No slang, no exclamation, no irony — but also no corporate distance. The register is that of an adult who is genuinely interested in what they make.
- **Address.** No "I", no "we" in marketing surfaces (rare exception: signed manifesto / letters). The brand addresses an implied "you" only obliquely — most often it speaks about the object, the gesture, the passion. The French-language source uses no `tu`/`vous` direct address.
- **Casing.** Headlines and structural marks use **UPPERCASE** (`WATCH YOUR PASSION`, `01 INTRODUCTION`, `WYP X MHA`). Body copy is sentence case. Avoid Title Case.
- **Numerals.** Section numbers use a leading zero (`01`, `02`, `03`). Section references use a decimal (`1.0`, `2.1`, `7.3`). Dates use ISO/European dot format: `11.02.2026`.
- **Emoji.** Never. Not in product, not on social. Unicode glyphs used as system marks are limited to: `+`, `↓ ↑ → ←`, `×`, `®`, `©`, the bullet `•`.
- **Bilingual register.** The brand is born French (Geneva) but builds for international reach. French is the primary editorial register in current source material; English is used for the signature line and product-system terms.

### Examples (from source)

**Headline register**
> WATCH YOUR PASSION.
> Defined by What Moves You.
> La passion mérite d'être portée avec précision.

**Manifeste / poetic register** (short declarative lines)
> Nous ne portons pas le temps.
> Nous portons ce qui nous anime.

**Description register** (calm, factual, technical)
> Pensée pour s'adapter à tous les contextes, la montre WYP révèle différents niveaux de lecture selon l'environnement. La lumière, les contrastes et les détails participent à une expérience visuelle évolutive, à la fois fonctionnelle et expressive.

**System marks** (label-as-metadata)
> `[11.02.2026]` `[WYP]` `[FRANCAIS]` `[Version N_01]` `BRANDBOOK`

This bracketed metadata pattern is a strong WYP signature — small uppercase labels in square brackets used as document headers, captions, and section tags.

### Failure modes — do NOT
- ❌ "Get yours now!" — too commercial, too urgent
- ❌ "We're so excited to introduce…" — adolescent, demonstrative
- ❌ "The ultimate / the best / the only" — superlatives are not WYP
- ❌ Emoji 🔥⌚✨ in any product or marketing surface
- ❌ Long flowing paragraphs of "storytelling" — WYP states, it does not narrate

## 4. VISUAL FOUNDATIONS

### The three-color promise
WYP's palette is intentionally minimal: **noir (`#0B0B0C`) · argent (`#A3A3A3`) · blanc (`#F5F5F5`)**. Per the Brandguide: *"La couleur définit le ton… Construite autour du noir, du blanc et de l'argent, elle crée une base visuelle stable… [Pour le système WYP] laissez les collaborations apporter la couleur. Privilégiez les compositions monochromes."* Colour, when it appears, **belongs to the collaboration, never to the brand chrome**.

### Typography
- **One family: BDO Grotesk** across the board — display, body, UI, labels, micro-text.
- **Two working weights: Medium (500) and DemiBold (600)** for almost everything. Light/Bold/Black are situational only.
- **Tracking 0% as default**, very slightly negative on display, positive only on small uppercase labels.
- **Line height tight on display (0.8–1.0)**, comfortable on body (~1.45). Spec: 150/120, 60/60, 24/30, 12.8/15.
- **UPPERCASE** for display, structural marks, document metadata, navigation. Sentence case for body.

### Layout & grid
- **12-column modular grid** with constant gutters and consistent margins.
- **Left alignment is the default.** Right and centre alignment are reserved for explicit composition moments.
- **Hierarchy first.** Blocks of content are clearly hierarchised; nothing is decoratively placed.
- **Repetition as rhythm.** The Horizon line (logo bar) is re-used as a structural rule throughout layouts to set rhythm; the half-circle is introduced *occasionally* as a punctuation moment, not as decoration.
- **Empty space is content.** Generous negative space is a defining characteristic. The brand prefers a single image and a single block of text on a page over multi-column density.

### Backgrounds
- **Flat noir or flat blanc.** No gradients. No noise. No textures. The two backgrounds correspond to the jour / nuit duality of the product.
- **Full-bleed editorial photography** is the only acceptable non-flat background — and only when the photo carries the composition itself (see imagery treatment below).
- **No illustrated backgrounds, no patterns, no repeated tiling.** The Horizon line acts as the only repeating motif, and it is structural, not decorative.

### Animation
- **Reveal, don't bounce.** WYP's signature motion is *révélation* — slow opacity / luminance fades tied to the jour-nuit concept (~600–900ms, ease-out).
- **No spring physics, no bounces, no rubber-band.** Easings are cubic, asymmetric, restrained.
- **Hover transitions: 120ms.** State transitions: 220ms. Reveals: 600–900ms.

### Hover states
- **Opacity-down to ~55%** on links and the logotype. The brand never uses tinted hover colors (no "darker blue", no "lighter grey").
- **On buttons:** background flips noir↔blanc (full inversion). No micro-tints.
- **On cards:** the only hover affordance is a subtle 1-pixel hairline appearing/strengthening, or the image scaling 1.02× over 480ms.

### Press / active states
- **Opacity drop to 0.4**, no scale-down, no shadow inset. WYP does not "press" — it switches state.

### Borders & rules
- **1px hairlines in `--line`** for structure, dividers, table rules.
- **1px structural rules in `--line-strong`** (= the brand black) for editorial section breaks.
- **No double borders, no inner glows, no border-radius accents.**
- Default `border-radius` is **0**. Cards, inputs, buttons, panels are all squared. Pill capsules (`999px`) appear *only* on collaboration chips / tags / numbered series badges.

### Shadows
- **Used sparingly.** WYP relies on contrast and structure rather than depth.
- A single subtle elevation token (`--shadow-2`) for floating menus / modals.
- **No inner shadows, no neumorphic effects, no soft drop-shadows behind cards.**

### Transparency & blur
- **Sparingly.** A 16–24px backdrop blur on the nav bar over imagery is acceptable.
- The brand prefers **solid panels over translucent overlays**.
- "Protection gradients" over imagery (for text legibility) are acceptable: a noir→transparent gradient from the bottom, never radial, never rainbow.

### Imagery — direction & treatment
From section 6 of the Brandguide:
- **Monochrome dominance.** Black-and-white or near-monochrome is the default. When a collaboration introduces colour, it occupies a defined zone, not the whole image.
- **Strong, controlled contrast.** Deep noir, structural greys, cold whites, contrast whites. The brand calls out four palette zones in imagery: `NOIR PROFOND · ARGENT · GRIS STRUCTURE · BLANC FROID · BLANC CONTRASTE`.
- **Directional, minimalist lighting.** Light is used to reveal form, not to flatter.
- **Tactile materiality.** Brushed metal, glass, leather strap textures, dust on concrete — physical surfaces matter. Imagery shows the texture of the world.
- **Centered or strongly-axis-aligned compositions.** Subject lives on a clear vertical or horizontal axis.
- **Subtle film grain is acceptable** on editorial photography. Heavy effects are not.

### Corner radii
| Token | Value | Used on |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `--radius-0` | 0 | default — every surface |
| `--radius-sm` | 2px | inputs, small interactive surfaces |
| `--radius-md` | 4px | tooltip, menu items |
| `--radius-lg` | 999px | collaboration tags & numbered-series chips only |

### Cards
- **No shadow, no rounded corners.** WYP cards are flat rectangles with a single 1px hairline border (or no border, against a noir/blanc field).
- The image fills edge-to-edge. Padding belongs to the surrounding grid, not inside the card.
- Card metadata sits *below* the image in a flat block (overline + title + price/code), not overlaid on top.

### Fixed elements
- **Navigation** is fixed top, full-width, transparent over hero imagery, becoming solid on scroll past the fold.
- **Menu, cart, search** sit right-aligned. The logomark or full lockup sits centred or left.
- **No fixed CTAs, no sticky banners, no cookie strips** floating in the corner. If consent is required, it occupies the full bottom edge as a solid bar.

## 5. ICONOGRAPHY

WYP **does not use a conventional UI icon library**. The brand-guide deliberately under-specifies iconography because the visual language relies on type and structure rather than glyph systems.

In practice, this system uses three layers:

1. **Brand marks as iconography.** The Horizon symbol (`assets/logos/WYP_ICON_NOIR.png`) — a horizontal bar and a half-circle — is *the* primary brand glyph. It is used at any scale, including favicon scale. Pair it with the logotype (`WYP_LOGOTYPE_*.png`) or use it standalone.
2. **A small set of UI utility icons** (search, menu, close, arrow, cart, plus/minus). These are not in the brandbook. We substitute **Lucide** (1.5px stroke, line-only, no fills) via CDN for these UI needs because Lucide's geometric line-only character matches WYP's structural aesthetic.
   - 🚩 **Substitution flag** — Lucide is *not* the official WYP icon set. Hyperbien should specify or commission an icon family if production needs more than utility navigation glyphs.
3. **Editorial system marks** in text: `+`, `→`, `↓`, `↑`, `×`, `®`, `©`. These are unicode glyphs set in BDO Grotesk and used directly in copy as labels and section markers. Examples from the brandbook: `+ Maintenir le contraste`, `↓ (DETAILS)`, `01 → 02`. **This is a deliberate WYP signature** — text glyphs doing work that designers usually delegate to icons.

**No emoji** — anywhere, ever. **No multicolor icons**, no filled glyphs, no rounded "friendly" icon sets. **No animated icons** beyond the brand's own jour-nuit reveal.

A "moon" PNG (`assets/icons/wyp_lune_noir.png`, `assets/icons/icon_lune.png`) was provided in the source materials and is included for the jour-nuit theme toggle affordance.

## 7. Caveats

- **No production codebase or Figma file** was provided. Component-level choices (button paddings, input heights, table densities, etc.) are inferred from the brand-guide's principles, not lifted from real product.
- **One typeface, with a substitution risk.** BDO Grotesk is the official face. There is no listed Google Fonts equivalent in the source; we ship the official woff2 files in `fonts/`. If a deployment cannot serve those, a stylistically reasonable fallback is **Inter** or **Helvetica Neue** — but neither has BDO Grotesk's distinctive curved `P`.
- **Iconography is under-specified** in the source. Lucide is used as a substitute; flag this to the brand owner before production.
- **Only one product surface** has a UI kit (marketing site) since the brand is pre-launch and no other product surface is described in source.
- **The brand is bilingual** (FR primary, EN signature). Copy in the kit defaults to French to mirror the source.
